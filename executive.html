Executive Branch

Governor of Texas

The Governor is the chief executive officer of the State of Texas. The Governor is responsible for enforcing state laws, directing executive agencies, serving as commander-in-chief of state military forces, and representing Texas at the state and national level.

Lieutenant Governor

The Lieutenant Governor presides over the Texas Senate and plays a central role in shaping legislation, committee assignments, and state policy priorities.

Attorney General

The Attorney General serves as the chief legal officer of Texas, representing the state in legal matters, providing opinions on state law, and enforcing consumer protection and criminal statutes.
